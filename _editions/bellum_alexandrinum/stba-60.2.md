               <tei-p xml:id="d2e31874" id="d2e31874" data-origname="p">In chapter 60 the rebellion against Cassius has reached a point where armed
                  conflict seems inevitable. Of the five legions originally controlled by Cassius
                  two and a half have mutinied (57.1–5) and put themselves under the leadership of
                  the quaestor M. Marcellus (59.1). Marcellus’ forces are based in Corduba itself,
                  Cassius’ are on high ground across the Baetis River, about four miles distant
                  (59.1). Cassius has taken the initiative by ravaging Cordoban fields and buildings
                  outside the city (59.2), and Marcellus’ troops demand action (60.1). In the
                  present sentence Marcellus does act, but with considerable reluctance, the grounds
                  for which are only partly clear in the text as transmitted, which is printed by
                     <tei-ref target="bibliography-modern-editions.html#Nipperdey">Nipperdey</tei-ref> and other editors (here with
                  Nipperdey’s punctuation):</tei-p>

               <tei-quote rend="blockquote" xml:id="d2e31881" id="d2e31881" data-origname="quote"> Marcellus, cum confligere miserrimum putaret, quod et
                  uictoris et uicti detrimentum ad eundem Caesarem esset redundaturum, neque suae
                  potestatis <tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e31883" id="d2e31883" data-origname="hi">esset,</tei-hi> legiones Baetim traducit aciemque
                  instruit.</tei-quote>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e31887" id="d2e31887" data-origname="p">This <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31889" id="d2e31889" data-origname="foreign">cum</tei-foreign> clause, like others in the <tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e31892" id="d2e31892" data-origname="hi">Bellum Alexandrinum</tei-hi>, is complex.<tei-note place="foot" xml:id="d2e31895" id="d2e31895" data-origname="note">See,
                     e.g., 34.2, 35.2, 36.3, 39.1, 40.2, 42.3, 44.1, 44.3, 63.4.</tei-note> It contains
                  two verbs, <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31898" id="d2e31898" data-origname="foreign">putaret</tei-foreign> (governing indirect statement)
                  and <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31901" id="d2e31901" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>, connected by <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31905" id="d2e31905" data-origname="foreign">neque</tei-foreign>, and an inset <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31908" id="d2e31908" data-origname="foreign">quod</tei-foreign> clause
                  including an emphatic genitive construction <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31911" id="d2e31911" data-origname="foreign">et uictoris et
                     uicti</tei-foreign>. Plus, one has to extract <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31914" id="d2e31914" data-origname="foreign">confligere</tei-foreign> from the indirect statement dependent on <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31917" id="d2e31917" data-origname="foreign">putaret</tei-foreign> to serve as the subject of <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31920" id="d2e31920" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>. The syntax is perhaps tolerable, although the
                     <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31924" id="d2e31924" data-origname="foreign">variatio</tei-foreign> in the modifiers of <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31927" id="d2e31927" data-origname="foreign">confligere</tei-foreign> is more suited to the style of Tacitus than
                  to that of Incertus. However, the meaning of <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31930" id="d2e31930" data-origname="foreign">suae
                     potestatis esset</tei-foreign> is unclear: does it allude to Marcellus’ probable
                  inability to control his troops? This would align with the order Cassius gave him
                  earlier, to retain control of Corduba (57.4 <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31933" id="d2e31933" data-origname="foreign">ut
                     eam</tei-foreign> [<tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e31936" id="d2e31936" data-origname="hi">sc</tei-hi>. <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31939" id="d2e31939" data-origname="foreign">Cordubam</tei-foreign>] <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e31943" id="d2e31943" data-origname="foreign">in potestate retineret</tei-foreign>)

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32050" id="d2e32050" data-origname="p">This emendation simplies the syntax of <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32052" id="d2e32052" data-origname="foreign">confligere</tei-foreign>
                  by putting it in a single accusative/infinitive clause with both of its
                  predicates, <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32055" id="d2e32055" data-origname="foreign">miserrimum</tei-foreign> and <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32058" id="d2e32058" data-origname="foreign">suae potestatis</tei-foreign>. But it does not really solve any of the other
                  problems mentioned above, and the present infinitive seems distinctly odd with the
                  second predicate, which is in effect a prediction about a specific future (note
                     <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32061" id="d2e32061" data-origname="foreign">si … facultas esset</tei-foreign> in 61.4) rather than a
                  generalization about conflict.<tei-note place="foot" xml:id="d2e32064" id="d2e32064" data-origname="note"><tei-ref target="bibliography-modern-editions.html#Kübler1896a">Kübler</tei-ref> (1896, <tei-ref target="https://hdl.handle.net/2027/osu.32435027192681?urlappend=%3Bseq=21" xml:id="d2e32068" id="d2e32068" data-origname="ref">XIX</tei-ref>) suggested a supplement based on 61.4 (quoted above), which he
                     put into the text after the recentiores’ <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32071" id="d2e32071" data-origname="foreign">esse</tei-foreign>:
                        <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32074" id="d2e32074" data-origname="foreign">Marcellus cum confligere miserrimum putaret … neque
                        suae potestatis esse <tei-supplied reason="lost" xml:id="d2e32076" id="d2e32076" data-origname="supplied">uideret militibus pugnandi
                           cupidissimis resistere</tei-supplied>, legiones Baetim traducit aciemque
                        instruit</tei-foreign>. However, his supplement moves <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32080" id="d2e32080" data-origname="foreign">esse</tei-foreign> into a new indirect statement governed by <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32083" id="d2e32083" data-origname="foreign">uideret</tei-foreign>. But it is hard to see how this supplement
                     would have fallen out, and why a scribe would alter the perfectly construable
                        <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32087" id="d2e32087" data-origname="foreign">esse</tei-foreign> to <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32090" id="d2e32090" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>.</tei-note></tei-p>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32095" id="d2e32095" data-origname="p">Another fairly simple repair was suggested by Ciacconius, who added an infinitive
                  after <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32097" id="d2e32097" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>. <tei-ref target="bibliography-modern-editions.html#Jungermann">Jungermann</tei-ref>’s report of the repair (1606, <tei-ref target="https://hdl.handle.net/2027/uc1.31822038197299?urlappend=%3Bseq=865" xml:id="d2e32103" id="d2e32103" data-origname="ref">333</tei-ref>) does not indicate whether Ciacconius intended the verb to be part
                  of the <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32106" id="d2e32106" data-origname="foreign">cum</tei-foreign> clause (with <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32109" id="d2e32109" data-origname="foreign">neque</tei-foreign> connecting <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32113" id="d2e32113" data-origname="foreign">putaret</tei-foreign> and
                     <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32116" id="d2e32116" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>) or part of the <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32119" id="d2e32119" data-origname="foreign">quod</tei-foreign> clause (with <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32122" id="d2e32122" data-origname="foreign">neque</tei-foreign> connecting
                     <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32125" id="d2e32125" data-origname="foreign">esset redundaturum</tei-foreign> and <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32128" id="d2e32128" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>):</tei-p>

               <tei-quote rend="blockquote" xml:id="d2e32132" id="d2e32132" data-origname="quote">Marcellus cum confligere miserrimum putaret, quod et
                  uictoris et uicti detrimentum ad eundem Caesarem esset redundaturum, neque suae
                  potestatis <tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e32134" id="d2e32134" data-origname="hi"><tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32135" id="d2e32135" data-origname="hi">esset</tei-hi></tei-hi>
                  <tei-supplied reason="lost" xml:id="d2e32138" id="d2e32138" data-origname="supplied"><tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e32139" id="d2e32139" data-origname="hi">uetare uel prohibere</tei-hi></tei-supplied>,
                  legiones Baetim traducit aciemque instruit.</tei-quote>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32143" id="d2e32143" data-origname="p">or</tei-p>

               <tei-quote rend="blockquote" xml:id="d2e32145" id="d2e32145" data-origname="quote">Marcellus cum confligere miserrimum putaret, quod et
                  uictoris et uicti detrimentum ad eundem Caesarem esset redundaturum neque suae
                  potestatis <tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e32147" id="d2e32147" data-origname="hi">esset </tei-hi><tei-supplied reason="lost" xml:id="d2e32149" id="d2e32149" data-origname="supplied"><tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e32150" id="d2e32150" data-origname="hi">uetare <tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32152" id="d2e32152" data-origname="hi">uel</tei-hi>
                  prohibere</tei-hi></tei-supplied>, legiones Baetim traducit aciemque instruit.</tei-quote>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32157" id="d2e32157" data-origname="p">In the <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32159" id="d2e32159" data-origname="foreign">cum</tei-foreign> clause <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32162" id="d2e32162" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign> requires an awkward shift from concessive sense (“although he
                  thought”) to causal (“since it was not in his power to prevent”). In the <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32165" id="d2e32165" data-origname="foreign">quod</tei-foreign> clause it is something of a non sequitur after
                     <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32168" id="d2e32168" data-origname="foreign">confligere miserrimum putaret</tei-foreign>.</tei-p>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32172" id="d2e32172" data-origname="p"><tei-ref target="bibliography-secondary-sources.html#Larsen">Larsen</tei-ref> (1886, <tei-ref target="https://hdl.handle.net/2027/nnc2.ark:/13960/t3cz4fm0n?urlappend=%3Bseq=22" xml:id="d2e32176" id="d2e32176" data-origname="ref">25</tei-ref>) left the matter of what was not in Marcellus’ power open, placing a
                  lacuna after <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32179" id="d2e32179" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>:</tei-p>

               <tei-quote rend="blockquote" xml:id="d2e32184" id="d2e32184" data-origname="quote">Marcellus cum confligere miserrimum putaret, quod et
                  uictoris et uicti detrimentum ad eundem Caesarem esset redundaturum, neque suae
                  potestatis <tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e32186" id="d2e32186" data-origname="hi">esset <tei-gap reason="lost" xml:id="d2e32188" id="d2e32188" data-origname="gap" data-empty="">​</tei-gap></tei-hi>, legiones Baetim
                  traducit aciemque instruit.<tei-note place="foot" xml:id="d2e32190" id="d2e32190" data-origname="note"><tei-ref target="bibliography-modern-editions.html#Klotz">Klotz</tei-ref>
                     (1927, ad loc.) and <tei-ref target="bibliography-modern-editions.html#Andrieu">Andrieu</tei-ref> (1954, ad loc.) oddly
                     print the recentiores’ <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32197" id="d2e32197" data-origname="foreign">esse</tei-foreign> and Larsen’s
                     lacuna.</tei-note>
               </tei-quote>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32202" id="d2e32202" data-origname="p">As a supplement one could imagine, say, a second <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32204" id="d2e32204" data-origname="foreign">quod</tei-foreign> clause explaining the second verb in the <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32207" id="d2e32207" data-origname="foreign">cum</tei-foreign> clause: e.g., <tei-supplied reason="lost" xml:id="d2e32210" id="d2e32210" data-origname="supplied"><tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32211" id="d2e32211" data-origname="foreign">quod belli imperium non obtineret</tei-foreign></tei-supplied>.<tei-note place="foot" xml:id="d2e32214" id="d2e32214" data-origname="note">For <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32216" id="d2e32216" data-origname="foreign">belli imperium</tei-foreign> cf. <tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32219" id="d2e32219" data-origname="hi">BG</tei-hi> 2.4.6 <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32222" id="d2e32222" data-origname="foreign">belli imperium sibi
                        postulare</tei-foreign>; for <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32225" id="d2e32225" data-origname="foreign">imperium obtinere</tei-foreign>
                     cf. 4.2 <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32228" id="d2e32228" data-origname="foreign">omne imperium obtinebat</tei-foreign> (sc. <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32232" id="d2e32232" data-origname="foreign">Arsinoe</tei-foreign>), 15.2 <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32235" id="d2e32235" data-origname="foreign">imperium
                        classis obtineret</tei-foreign> (sc. <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32238" id="d2e32238" data-origname="foreign">Euphranor</tei-foreign>); for the connection between <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32241" id="d2e32241" data-origname="foreign">imperium</tei-foreign> and <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32244" id="d2e32244" data-origname="foreign">potestas</tei-foreign>
                     <tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32247" id="d2e32247" data-origname="hi">BG</tei-hi> 7.4.6–7 <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32251" id="d2e32251" data-origname="foreign">ad eum</tei-foreign> (sc.
                        <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32254" id="d2e32254" data-origname="foreign">Vercingetorigem</tei-foreign>) <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32257" id="d2e32257" data-origname="foreign">defertur imperium. qua oblata potestate</tei-foreign>, etc.</tei-note> But this
                  explanation adds very little, especially by comparison with the first <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32261" id="d2e32261" data-origname="foreign">quod</tei-foreign> clause, which justifies the striking word <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32265" id="d2e32265" data-origname="foreign">miserrimum</tei-foreign>.</tei-p>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32269" id="d2e32269" data-origname="p">Another possibility would be to find a supplement that would place <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32271" id="d2e32271" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign> in the extant <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32274" id="d2e32274" data-origname="foreign">quod</tei-foreign> clause, along the lines of Ciacconius’ emendation, discussed
                  above. As an <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32277" id="d2e32277" data-origname="foreign">exempli gratia</tei-foreign> supplement based on
                  the subsequent course of events we considered:</tei-p>

               <tei-quote rend="blockquote" xml:id="d2e32281" id="d2e32281" data-origname="quote">Marcellus cum confligere miserrimum putaret, quod et
                  uictoris et uicti detrimentum ad eundem Caesarem esset redundaturum neque suae
                  potestatis <tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e32283" id="d2e32283" data-origname="hi">esset </tei-hi><tei-supplied reason="lost" xml:id="d2e32285" id="d2e32285" data-origname="supplied"><tei-hi rend="underline" xml:id="d2e32286" id="d2e32286" data-origname="hi">receptus</tei-hi></tei-supplied>, legiones Baetim traducit.</tei-quote>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32290" id="d2e32290" data-origname="p">With this supplement Marcellus would consider combat utterly wretched “because the
                  loss for both the victor and defeated would similarly redound on Caesar and
                  retreat was not in his power.” The phrase <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32292" id="d2e32292" data-origname="foreign">esset
                     receptus</tei-foreign> could be misconstrued as a pluperfect verb form, of course,
                  but the same is true at <tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32295" id="d2e32295" data-origname="hi">BC</tei-hi> 1.45.6 <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32298" id="d2e32298" data-origname="foreign">Hac nostris erat receptus</tei-foreign> and <tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32301" id="d2e32301" data-origname="hi">BC</tei-hi> 3.45.5
                     <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32304" id="d2e32304" data-origname="foreign">erat per decliue receptus</tei-foreign>. However, the
                  difficulties of the terrain are not mentioned until later in the narrative
                  (60.4–5, esp. <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32308" id="d2e32308" data-origname="foreign">cum … quid transitus fluminis uitii
                     difficultatisque haberet cognitum esset</tei-foreign>), and the two explanations
                  for <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32311" id="d2e32311" data-origname="foreign">miserrimum</tei-foreign> are both strangely dissimilar and
                  strangely arranged, with the proximate and practical worry about retreat placed
                  after the future and general worry about what the battle would cost Caesar. And
                  even if one solves the latter problem by placing a comma before <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32314" id="d2e32314" data-origname="foreign">neque</tei-foreign> and making <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32317" id="d2e32317" data-origname="foreign">esset</tei-foreign>
                  <tei-supplied reason="lost" xml:id="d2e32320" id="d2e32320" data-origname="supplied"><tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32321" id="d2e32321" data-origname="foreign">receptus</tei-foreign></tei-supplied>
                  parallel to <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32324" id="d2e32324" data-origname="foreign">putaret</tei-foreign>, the fact that we don’t know
                  about the difficulties of the terrain gives pause.</tei-p>

               <tei-p xml:id="d2e32328" id="d2e32328" data-origname="p">In the end we decided that no supplement (and hence no lacuna) is needed here, and
                  that the transmitted text, despite the awkward syntax of <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32330" id="d2e32330" data-origname="foreign">confligere</tei-foreign> and the ambiguity of <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32333" id="d2e32333" data-origname="foreign">potestatis</tei-foreign>, was acceptable. Marcellus’ worry about the limits of his
                  authority aligns well his subsequent decision to withdraw his troops at the first
                  opportunity (60.3) and with Caesar’s obiter dictum at <tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32336" id="d2e32336" data-origname="hi">BC</tei-hi>
                  3.51.4: <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32339" id="d2e32339" data-origname="foreign">aliae … sunt legati partes atque
                     imperatoris</tei-foreign>. Marcellus’ loyalty to Caesar is stressed at 59.1, his
                  deference to authority at 63.2 (<tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32342" id="d2e32342" data-origname="foreign">huic</tei-foreign> [<tei-hi rend="italic" xml:id="d2e32346" id="d2e32346" data-origname="hi">sc</tei-hi>. <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32349" id="d2e32349" data-origname="foreign">Lepido</tei-foreign>] <tei-foreign xml:lang="la" xml:id="d2e32352" id="d2e32352" data-origname="foreign">uenienti sine dubitatione Marcellus se credit</tei-foreign>), and his
                  reluctance to fight is mentioned more forcefully than in the present passage at
                  61.4 (quoted above). The repairs reported in the apparatus indicate our lingering
                  dissatisfaction with the paradosis and suggest possible approaches for improving
                  it.</tei-p>
